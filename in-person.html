In Person work model

Introduction

In a survey that was conducted by the US research institute Gensler Research Institute, around 90% of employees prefer to go back to work in an office environment. Employees who work in offices need to have different skills such as the ability to exchange opinions, face to face communication, and work as a team. Working from home can lead to stress as the cohabiting and working places are similar. Although remote work has been dominantly applied starting from the pandemic time, many professionals are looking forward to returning to their office environment, as they miss working together with other colleagues, sharing their daily life, and getting inspired in spaces specially designed to stimulate their creativity.

Pros of In-person work environment

  1. Conduct face to face meetings

    2. It is very essential for certain projects or works to conduct face-to-face meetings. Arranging personal meetings with our important clients can help us to easily market our ideas or proposals as our clients will have a sense of engagement and being part of the process. Face-to-face meetings also help our clients to see how we perform safely and naturally in a professional manner.

  2. Better distribution of workspace

    3. On certain occasions, office work includes doing tasks in a highly optimized and functional workspace. This type of workspace is interesting as it allows the free circulation of employees in a fluid manner. In this way, it is easier to improve and increase the interaction of workers. Additionally, it also makes workers active on their duty as they will not be sitting on a chair for long hours like in remote work areas.

Cons of In-person work environment

  1. Lack of Privacy

    2. Introverted people may not like working in an office environment as a team. This is going to be a great disadvantage if we work in open spaces and offices, as many introverted employees can end up feeling uncomfortable. As a result of this, the overall productivity of the team is more likely to be below the expected value.

  2. Increased stress and/or anxiety

    3. An employee is more likely to feel under pressure when working in an office environment either by our managers or other colleagues. This is going to result in lower productivity and capacity to work as it causes stress on the worker. They might result in rapid burnout of employees if not managed properly.
